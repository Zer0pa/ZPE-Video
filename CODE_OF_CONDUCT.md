We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in ZPE
Video a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste,
colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open,
welcoming, and evidence-led community.

---

<p>
  <img src=".github/assets/readme/section-bars/our-standards.svg" alt="OUR STANDARDS" width="100%">
</p>

### Expected behaviour

- Demonstrating empathy and respect toward other contributors
- Giving and accepting evidence-backed feedback
- Treating negative results and retired surfaces as legitimate contributions
- Correcting mistakes directly instead of narrating around them
- Protecting the integrity of the proof corpus and the repo boundary

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Inflating technical claims beyond their evidenced scope
- Dismissing, hiding, or attacking negative findings because they are inconvenient
- Submitting contributions designed to obscure the real artifact trail
- Sustained bad-faith disputes without counter-evidence

---

<p>
  <img src=".github/assets/readme/section-bars/evidence-discipline-as-community-norm.svg" alt="EVIDENCE DISCIPLINE AS COMMUNITY NORM" width="100%">
</p>

ZPE Video operates under falsifiability-first principles. In practice:

- Claims require evidence.
- Negative results are protected.
- Scope inflation is treated as an integrity problem, not a style issue.
- If a surface failed, the community norm is to preserve the failure and
  pivot honestly.
